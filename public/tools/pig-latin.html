Why Pig Latin?

Pig Latin is a language game played in English-speaking countries since at least the early 1900s. It's not actually Latin — the name was a joke about it sounding fancy and unintelligible. Children use it as a "secret code" between friends; adults occasionally use it for harmless code-words.

FAQ

What are the Pig Latin rules?

Vowel-start = +way. Consonant-start = move the cluster, +ay. Example: egg → eggway, strong → ongstray.

Does it preserve capitalisation?

Yes — initial-capital, ALL-CAPS, and trailing punctuation are all preserved.
